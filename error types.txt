Assuming deductive closure (all of the possible additional statements have already been derived, e.g. domain and range constraints of inverse properties)

1. Disjoint properties between the same pair of entities
    1.1. Disjointness between two properties -- hasBrother and isChildOf
    1.2. Disjointness between a property and a property chain -- isSiblingOf (:hasParent and :isParentOf) and isParentOf
    1.3. Disjointness between two property chains -- isUncleOf (:isBrotherOf and :isParentOf)
                                                     and isAuntOf (:isBrotherOf and :isParentOf)
2. Disjoint classes assigned to the same entity
    2.1. Disjointness of two regular classes
    2.2. Disjointness of a blank class (e.g., an owl:Restriction) and a regular class
    2.3. Disjointness of two blank classes
3. Violation of domain or range constraints of a relation
4. Irreflexivity Violation: A property is owl:IrreflexiveProperty, but the data contains a triple $(A, P, A)$
5. Asymmetry Violation: $(A, P, B)$ and $(B, P, A)$ both exist for an owl:AsymmetricProperty
6. Max Cardinality Violation: A node has 3 values for a property restricted to maxQualifiedCardinality 2.
7. Functional Property Violation: A FunctionalProperty (like hasBirthMother) points to two distinct individuals who are not the same.
